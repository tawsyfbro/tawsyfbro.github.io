ABOUT METROPOLITAN UNIVERSITY

Metropolitan University is a leading private university in Bangladesh, committed to providing quality education and fostering academic excellence. Established with a vision to transform education and create future leaders, the university offers a diverse range of programs across various disciplines.

Located in Sylhet, Bangladesh, Metropolitan University has been at the forefront of higher education for over two decades. Our institution is dedicated to nurturing intellectual growth, promoting research, and preparing students for successful careers in their chosen fields.

With state-of-the-art facilities, experienced faculty members, and a vibrant campus community, Metropolitan University provides an enriching learning environment that encourages innovation, critical thinking, and personal development.

Vision and Mission

Our Vision

To emerge as a distinguished teaching and research university recognized around the globe through innovative education, creation and application of knowledge, and community engagement.

Our Mission

To provide our students with globally compatible tertiary education characterized by academic excellence in a range of subjects pertinent to present and future social needs; to impart the necessary lessons on moral values, ethics, self-respect, and patriotism; and to establish a stimulating learning environment where they can prepare themselves for pursuing their academic, personal, and career goals.

Our Core Values

Innovation

We inspire innovative thinking and nurture curiosity to develop cutting-edge solutions in engineering, technology, and research.

Integrity

We uphold honesty, transparency, and ethical responsibility in every academic, professional, and social endeavor.

Discipline

We believe that discipline builds excellence — encouraging dedication, punctuality, and professionalism among all members of our community.

Collaboration

We promote teamwork and shared learning to achieve academic and research goals through mutual respect and cooperation.

Global Vision

We prepare students to thrive in a global society through international exposure, cross-cultural understanding, and global competence.

Patriotism

We are devoted to national progress, inspiring our students to contribute to the sustainable development of Bangladesh and beyond.

Convocation

Metropolitan University upholds the long-standing academic tradition of convocation, celebrating the formal conferment of degrees with due festivity. To date, the University has held three convocations and now looks forward to its fourth.

First Convocation (22 April 2010, Jalalabad Gas Auditorium, Sylhet): Presided over by Education Minister Nurul Islam Nahid, MP, on behalf of the then Honourable President and Chancellor Md. Zillur Rahman. The ceremony was attended by distinguished guests including UGC Chairman Prof. Nazrul Islam, former Sylhet City Mayor Badar Uddin Ahmed Kamran, Board Chair Dr. Toufique Rahman Chowdhury, and Vice-Chancellor Prof. Md. Abdul Aziz. Former Bangladesh Bank Governor Prof. Md. Farashuddin delivered the Convocation Address. Degrees were conferred on 482 graduates; four received the Dean’s Award and one received the Chancellor’s Award for the highest CGPA.

Second Convocation (31 January 2015, Sylhet Sports Complex): Chaired again by Education Minister Nurul Islam Nahid, MP, on behalf of the Chancellor. The Convocation Address was delivered by Prof. Dr. Tapadhir Bhattacharya, former Vice-Chancellor of Assam University. Special guests included National Pay Commission Chairman Dr. Mohammad Farashuddin, former UGC Member Atful Hai Shibli, and Prof. Syed Manzoorul Islam. A total of 1,382 graduates received degrees; six meritorious graduates were awarded the Chancellor’s Gold Medal.

Third Convocation (4 August 2018, Permanent Campus, Bateshwar, Sylhet): Presided over by Education Minister Nurul Islam Nahid, MP, on behalf of the Honourable President of the People’s Republic of Bangladesh and Chancellor Md. Abdul Hamid. The Convocation Address was delivered by Prof. Syed Manzoorul Islam, Ekushey Padak recipient and former Professor of English, University of Dhaka. Degrees were conferred on 1,870 graduates (up to July 2018); 1,026 students registered and attended, and eight received the Chancellor’s Gold Medal.
